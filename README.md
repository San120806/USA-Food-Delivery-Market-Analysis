U.S. Food Delivery Market Competition Analysis
Business Problem

The U.S. food delivery industry is intensely competitive. Restaurants must carefully determine pricing strategies while ensuring customer satisfaction. This project evaluates how pricing and service quality influence demand using AI-driven methods.

Objectives

Segment the market using K-Means clustering

Evaluate price elasticity through Logistic Regression

Model price variation effects using an interactive elasticity simulator

Generate insights for revenue maximization

Dataset

Source: Kaggle

Key Findings

The majority of menu items are priced within the $5–$20 range.

The market is primarily concentrated in the mid-price segment.

Price has a slight negative impact on demand probability.

Higher ratings significantly increase the likelihood of demand.

Elasticity simulations indicate demand is relatively inelastic within typical price ranges.

The market structure aligns with characteristics of monopolistic competition.
